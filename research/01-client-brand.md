## 1. Product Details
- **Name:** Brake Checker Pro (formerly "Brake Light & Air Leak Checker Tool")
- **Price:** **$89.99** (existing market price — keep unless user changes)
- **What it is:** Adjustable telescoping steel rod with rubber-padded feet that wedges between the brake pedal and seat base, holding the pedal depressed so a solo driver can walk the truck/trailer to check brake lights and listen/look for air leaks.
- **Construction:** Lightweight steel, patented design, **Assembled in USA**.
- **Compatibility:** Works on all commercial trucks. Fits both free-swinging and floor-mounted brake pedals. Trailers too.

## 2. How It Works (Verbatim from site)
1. **Start the engine**, let air pressure build up completely.
2. **Place tool between the brake pedal and seat bracket** to hold pressure.
3. **Walk around the truck and trailer** to identify air leaks or brake light issues.
4. Catch problems **before DOT does**.

## 10. Fonts
- **Body & UI:** Jost (Google Fonts) — geometric sans, modern but slightly generic
- **Default fallback in places:** Times New Roman (likely a bug)
- **Recommendation:** Pair Jost (or upgrade to Inter) for body with a heavier industrial display like **Bebas Neue**, **Anton**, or **Druk Wide** for hero headlines. Trucking/industrial space rewards a slab or condensed display.

## 11. Brand Tone Analysis
- Direct, plain English
- Uses driver terminology authentically ("DOT officers", "free-swinging brake pedal", "out of service")
- Warning-flavored copy ("Without this tool drivers may hit the road unaware…")
- Slightly amateur copy patterns ("This isn't a tool you want it's a tool you NEED!")

**Recommendation:** Sharpen the voice. Keep the working-class authenticity. Drop punctuation errors. Lean into the DOT-citation pain point because that's the financial trigger.

## Key Insights for the Rebuild
1. **The product is real and good.** Patented, USA-assembled, single-driver solution. The story is genuinely compelling — it's just packaged like a template SMB site.
2. **The DOT angle is undersold.** "Out of service" is a $500–$2,000 event for a driver (lost loads, fines, downtime). Lead with that.
3. **The price ($89.99) pays for itself in one prevented ticket.** That ROI math must be front and center.
4. **Real product photography exists** — the cab-installed shots are excellent. They prove the product is real, not a render.
5. **The testimonials are obviously fake.** Replace.
6. **Founder isn't shown.** If client has a portrait, use it. "By drivers, for drivers" with a real face is 10x stronger.
